Repairing and Repainting My Car

Project Introduction

This project documents my experience repairing and repainting my car after it was damaged in two different incidents. The car was first side swiped, which left noticeable scratches and marks on one side. Later, my mom also scratched the car, adding more damage to the same general area. Instead of immediately taking the car to a professional body shop, I decided to use the situation as a chance to learn something new while also trying to save money. Body shop repairs can be expensive even for damage that looks mostly cosmetic. Because of that, I became interested in understanding what actually goes into fixing a damaged area. I started learning about the basic steps involved in auto body repair, including cleaning the area, sanding, taping off the section, applying primer, and preparing for paint. As I worked through the project, I got to see how much patience and attention to detail are involved in even a small repair. My goal was not to make the car look absolutely perfect like a professional shop might, but to improve the damaged area and learn useful skills in the process. This website shows the background of the project, some images from the repair, and a description of the steps I followed as I worked on the car.
