Repairing and Repainting My Car

Project Introduction

This project documents my experience repairing and repainting my car after it was damaged in two different incidents. The car was first side swiped, which left noticeable scratches and damage on one side of the vehicle. Later, my mom accidentally scratched the car as well, which added more cosmetic damage. Instead of immediately taking the car to a professional body shop, I decided to try fixing the damage myself. I saw it as an opportunity to learn new skills while also saving money. Car body work and paint repair can be very expensive when done professionally. Even relatively small scratches or dents can cost hundreds or thousands of dollars to repair. Because of this, I became interested in learning how these repairs are actually done. I started researching the tools, materials, and techniques used by auto body shops and hobbyists. This included learning about sanding, priming, applying paint, and clear coating. Throughout this project I experimented with different methods to improve the appearance of the damaged areas. My goal was not necessarily to achieve a perfect professional result, but to gain hands-on experience and understand the process. This website shows some of the steps I took and what I learned while working on the car.
